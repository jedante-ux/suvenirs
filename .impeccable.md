## Design Context

### Users
Corporate clients (HR managers, office managers, marketing teams) in Chile looking to purchase personalized gifts for employees, events, or client appreciation. They visit the site during work hours, often comparing options quickly. They need to browse products, build quotes, and share them with decision-makers. Speed and clarity matter — they're busy professionals, not browsing for fun.

### Brand Personality
**Playful, Modern, Bold** — Suvenirs is not a stuffy corporate supplier. The brand embraces its hot pink identity with confidence. It's the kind of company that makes corporate gifting feel exciting rather than obligatory. The personality walks the line between professional credibility and creative energy.

### Emotional Goals
- **Delight & Surprise**: "This is way nicer than I expected for a gift company" — the interface should exceed expectations at every turn, making visitors feel they've discovered something special.

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Visual tone**: Apple-like polish meets playful personality. Clean, premium product showcasing with beautiful imagery, but infused with the brand's bold pink energy. Not cold minimalism — warm, inviting, and confident.
- **Theme**: Light mode primary. The hot pink (#E91E91) is the hero color — use it with intention, not everywhere.
- **Typography**: Poppins — geometric, modern, friendly. Weights should create clear hierarchy.
- **Anti-references**: Generic B2B catalog sites, cluttered e-commerce with banner ads, dark mode tech aesthetics, overly corporate/stiff layouts.

### Motion Direction
- **Subtle & Refined**: Animations should be barely noticeable but make everything feel smooth and polished. Like Apple — you don't notice the animations, you just notice everything feels right.
- No bouncy or elastic easing. Smooth exponential ease-out curves.
- Staggered reveals on page load, smooth state transitions, micro-feedback on interactions.

### Design Principles
1. **Polish over flash** — Every pixel should feel intentional. Prefer refined details over dramatic effects.
2. **Pink with purpose** — The bold brand color is a superpower when used with restraint. Let it punctuate, not overwhelm.
3. **Delight in the details** — Surprise users with quality in unexpected places: smooth transitions, thoughtful hover states, elegant loading.
4. **Clarity first** — Corporate buyers need to find products and build quotes fast. Never sacrifice usability for aesthetics.
5. **Warm professionalism** — Approachable enough to feel human, polished enough to feel trustworthy.
